I watched a lion yawn from about fifteen meters away and my first thought was "I need to send this to my mum."

Not "wow, the majesty of nature." Not "I am one with the wilderness." Just "mum would LOVE this." Which is, I think, the most honest thing I can tell you about what safari in Tanzania actually feels like. It's extraordinary and mundane at the same time. You're sitting in a Land Cruiser at 6am in the Serengeti, jaw on the floor, fumbling with your phone because the lighting is perfect and that elephant is RIGHT THERE, and also you kind of need to pee and your guide is already on to the next animal sighting.

Tanzania broke my brain. The Serengeti is so vast it makes you feel like a glitch in the landscape. Ngorongoro Crater looks like someone Photoshopped a zoo into a volcano. And then you leave the bush and fly to Zanzibar and suddenly you're eating fresh octopus on a beach in Stone Town while a guy tries to sell you a spice tour for the third time that day.

Dar es Salaam is its own kind of chaos. The traffic, the heat, the markets, the ferry terminal where everyone's shoving to get to Zanzibar. I loved it. (In hindsight. At the time I was mostly sweating and confused.)

Here's the connectivity reality: Tanzania works better than you'd expect in the cities and major safari areas. Dar, Arusha, Zanzibar, even parts of the Serengeti near lodges and camps have usable signal. Deep in the bush, between camps, on remote game drives far from any tower? Nothing. And honestly, that's fine. You don't need Instagram when there's a cheetah hunting 200 meters from your vehicle.

But you DO need data in the cities, at the airports, for booking ferries and transfers, for WhatsApp (which is how Tanzania runs), and for uploading those lion photos to make everyone back home jealous. An esim sorted before you land makes all of that work from the moment you touch down at Kilimanjaro International or Julius Nyerere. Having internet access and a mobile data plan ready to go means you stay connected from the second you clear customs. No hunting for a physical sim vendor, no registration hassle, no wasted time.

## How to Choose the Right eSIM for Tanzania

**How long is your trip?** The classic Tanzania itinerary is 7-10 days of safari (Serengeti, Ngorongoro, maybe Tarangire) plus 4-7 days in Zanzibar. That's roughly two to three weeks total. The 3GB/30-day plan from eSIMply at $9.95 covers this comfortably. The 5GB plan at $13.95 gives you breathing room for more photo uploads.

**How much data do you actually need?** On safari, you'll use less data than you think. Signal is intermittent in the parks, and you'll be too busy watching animals to scroll through anything. Zanzibar and Dar es Salaam is where the data gets used: maps, WhatsApp, booking ferries, uploading photos. 3-5GB covers a standard trip. 10GB if you're working remotely.

**Do you need unlimited data?** If you're working remotely from Zanzibar or Dar, eSIMply's unlimited plans are the only option on this list. The 7-day unlimited at $34.95 covers a week of video calls and heavy usage. Nobody else offers unlimited for Tanzania.

**Safari-specific advice:** Download offline Google Maps for your safari areas before you leave. Your guide will know where to go, but offline maps are useful for understanding distances and routes between parks. Don't rely on mobile data during game drives. You'll have signal at lodges and camps (usually), but not while bumping along dirt tracks at sunrise.

**What about Zanzibar specifically?** Coverage on Zanzibar island is good across Stone Town and the main beach areas (Nungwi, Kendwa, Paje). If you're travelling to Zanzibar from the mainland, your Tanzania esim works automatically since it's the same country. Remote eastern beaches can be spottier. Most guesthouses and hotels have Wi-Fi, but it varies wildly in quality. Having your own esim data as backup is worth it.

**Are there roaming charges with a travel esim in Tanzania?** No. That's the whole point. A travel esim or prepaid esim plan gives you a local data connection without international roaming charges from your home carrier. You buy the plan upfront and use it like a local. No surprise bills when you get home.
