## How to Choose the Right eSIM for the USA

**How long is your trip?** A week in New York or LA means 3-5GB is comfortable for Google Maps, messaging, and occasional streaming. A month-long road trip across multiple states, especially with remote work, means 10GB minimum. Unlimited plans start making sense if you're on video calls daily.

**Choose the best eSIM based on your data needs.** The right eSIM depends on how much data you'll actually use. If you're not sure, go slightly bigger. Running out of mobile data mid-navigation in rural Nevada is genuinely inconvenient.

**Are you staying in cities or going rural?** This actually matters in the US more than almost anywhere in Europe. Cell coverage drops off fast in national parks, mountain areas, and across large swaths of the midwest. If you're driving from coast to coast or spending time in genuinely remote areas, check which network your new eSIM prioritizes. Verizon generally wins for rural coverage. T-Mobile wins for 5G in cities.

**Do you need calls and SMS?** Most eSIMs for USA are data-only. In the US, this matters more than usual because Uber drivers, Airbnb hosts, and restaurant reservation systems often want to reach you by phone or text. eSIMply's esim plans with bundled calls and SMS solve this cleanly. Alternatively, WhatsApp over data handles most situations. You can make phone calls through apps without needing a local number.

**Coming from the United Kingdom or Europe?** Data roaming charges from UK or European carriers can hit $10-15 per day in the US. Even if your home plan mentions "included international roaming," there are usually strict fair-use caps and speed throttles. An eSIM for the USA is almost always better value. Some travelers also look at esim for europe and usa combo plans, but country-specific eSIMs tend to be better value per GB. Even Orange UK customers with roaming add-ons will save money with a dedicated USA eSIM.

**What about public Wi-Fi?** The US has a lot of public Wi-Fi at coffee shops, airports, and hotels. But relying on a random internet connection for anything sensitive (banking, email, work) is not great practice. A personal mobile data connection via eSIM is safer and more reliable. Saily adds an extra layer here with built-in VPN protection. Using your own connection instead of a public Wi-Fi hotspot is just smarter.

**Which US network is best for travelers?**

T-Mobile has the widest 5G coverage in the US and is generally strongest in cities. Verizon has the best rural coverage and is the choice for national parks and remote areas. Yes. All plans on this list support hotspot use. Useful if you're working from a laptop or sharing data with a travel companion. **How much data do I need for a US trip?**

A rough guide: 1-3GB works for a long weekend in a city with Wi-Fi at your accommodation. 5-10GB is right for one to two weeks of regular travel. 20GB+ covers a month-long road trip or extended remote work. If you're using Google Maps for navigation and streaming music or podcasts while driving, that burns through much data faster than you'd expect. **Can I use a USA eSIM in US national parks?**

No eSIM solves this completely.
