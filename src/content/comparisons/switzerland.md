## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do eSIMs work well in Switzerland?**

Yes, very well. Switzerland has excellent mobile infrastructure. Swisscom, Sunrise, and Salt all run strong 4G networks with growing 5G coverage in the cities. Swisscom has particularly good mountain coverage into the Alps. In the ski resorts and main Alpine destinations, coverage is solid. The Swiss rail network is famously good, and connectivity on trains is generally reliable.

**Is an eSIM cheaper than roaming in Switzerland?**

Yes. Switzerland is not in the EU, so EU free-roaming rules don't apply to most carriers. International roaming charges can run $15-30 per day or more. A 10GB eSIM valid for 30 days costs $16-22 depending on provider. The savings are significant for any stay longer than a day or two.

**How much data do I need for Switzerland?**

A week in Zurich and Geneva: 2-3GB. A week including Alpine day trips: 3-5GB. Two weeks touring the full country: 5-10GB. Remote work for a month: 10GB plan. Switzerland's excellent public transport system means you'll use maps constantly, which adds up.

**Is there eSIM coverage in Zermatt?**

Yes. Zermatt is well covered by Swisscom and other networks. The car-free village and the ski area have reliable 4G. Higher on the Matterhorn and Rothorn hiking areas, coverage can get intermittent. For navigation on high mountain routes, download offline maps.

**Can I use my eSIM on the Swiss railway network?**

Yes. Switzerland's trains are well covered. InterCity and Intercity Express routes between Zurich, Bern, Geneva, and Basel have solid connectivity throughout. Mountain rack railways and cogwheel trains to alpine destinations are covered in the valley but may lose signal in tunnels.

**How do I set up an eSIM for Switzerland?**

Purchase online, receive QR code by email or in-app, go to phone settings, add new eSIM, scan QR code. Under five minutes. Set activation for your Switzerland arrival date to preserve validity. Phone must be eSIM-compatible and carrier-unlocked.

**What Swiss networks do these eSIMs use?**

eSIMply connects to Swisscom, Sunrise, and Salt: all three major Swiss operators. Your eSIM automatically selects the strongest available signal. Swisscom is generally the best for alpine coverage.

**eSIMply or Nomad for Switzerland?**

For 1-5GB usage: eSIMply is cheaper. For 10GB: Nomad at $16.00 is about $6 cheaper than eSIMply's $21.95. For unlimited: Nomad's plans are significantly cheaper than eSIMply's. Check your likely data usage and compare at that tier.

**Can I use my Switzerland eSIM as a hotspot?**

Yes. All plans above support hotspot tethering. Useful if you're working from a Zurich café with your laptop, or sharing data with a travel companion. Just note that hotspot use draws down your data allowance faster than regular phone browsing.
