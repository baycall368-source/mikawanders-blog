## How to Choose the Right eSIM for a Turkey Trip

**How much data do you need?** A week in Istanbul with light use (Google Maps, messaging, some browsing) needs 2-3GB. Two weeks traveling between cities and sites, you want 5GB minimum. A month working remotely with video calls: go for the 10 GB plan and track your usage.

**Do you need unlimited data?** Most travelers don't. Turkey's connectivity is reliable enough that you're not constantly reaching for your phone as a backup. If you're streaming video all day or doing extended video calls, look at Nomad's unlimited data plans. Otherwise a 5GB or 10GB fixed plan from eSIMply covers most use cases at better value.

**Are you staying in Turkey only or going further?** Turkey-specific eSIMs give better value per GB than regional plans. If your trip continues outside Turkey into neighboring countries, Airalo or Roamless regional options start making more sense.

**Do you need a local Turkish number?** None of the providers above include a local phone number. Without a phone number you can still use WhatsApp, Google Maps, email, and most everyday travel apps. If you need to receive Turkish SMS (banking verification, local bookings via SMS), that requires a local SIM card or a provider that includes telephone number services.

**What about internet restrictions in Turkey?** Turkey has government restrictions on some platforms. Social media access is throttled at times. Many travelers and residents use VPNs. Turkey without a VPN can mean limited access to certain websites and apps. If you want a VPN layer on top of your mobile data, Saily's NordVPN background is worth considering.

**Can you use your primary sim card alongside an eSIM?** Yes. Most Samsung, iPhone, and Android phone models support dual SIM. Your primary SIM card stays in for calls and texts on your home number while the eSIM handles mobile data in Turkey. No roaming charges on your home plan, no switching. You stay connected on both lines.
