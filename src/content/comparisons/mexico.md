## How to Choose the Right eSIM for Mexico

**How long are you going and where?** A week in Mexico City or Cancun means 3GB is probably comfortable with wifi at your hotel in the evenings. Two weeks across multiple cities (CDMX to Oaxaca to the coast) means 5-10GB depending on how much you stream and navigate. A month working remotely as a digital nomad means 10GB minimum.

**Do you need unlimited data?** Most Mexico travelers don't, even if they think they do. Maps, messaging, Signal, and social media use won't get you to 10GB in two weeks unless you're streaming video constantly (Netflix and the like will chew through data fast). Unlimited plans are expensive in Mexico relative to fixed data. Unless you're doing video calls all day, a 10GB fixed plan is better value than any unlimited option on this list.

**Do you need a local phone number?** Most travelers don't. WhatsApp calls and SMS work fine for staying in touch without a Mexican telephone number. Where a local phone number matters: receiving verification codes from Mexican services, Uber pickup coordination, or local delivery notifications. If that applies, the Holafly eSIM includes a number on some plans.

**Are you staying in cities or going further out?** Coverage in Mexico City, Guadalajara, Oaxaca, Mérida, Playa del Carmen, and along the Yucatan coast is genuinely solid. Where it gets patchier is in the sierras, remote coastal areas, and small inland villages. For most tourist itineraries, this won't be an issue. Download offline maps before you leave the city.

**Mexico plus other countries?** Mexico-specific plans give better value per GB when you're staying put. If your trip extends to Guatemala, Belize, or back through the United States, regional plans from Airalo or Roamless's balance system start making more sense.

**Coming from the United States?** Your US roaming plan might include Mexico data, but check the fine print. Many US plans throttle speeds in Mexico or cap data after a small allowance. An eSIM eliminates roaming fees entirely and often gives you faster, more reliable mobile data than your home plan's roaming.

**Wi-Fi hotspot situation:** Most accommodation in Mexico has wifi. Cafés in tourist areas generally do too. But it's variable in quality and not something you want to depend on for navigation or work. Your own eSIM and internet connection is faster, more reliable, and more secure than a café router that's been rebooted twice since 2019.
