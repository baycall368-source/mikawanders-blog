## How to Choose the Right eSIM for Canada

**Accept that Canada is expensive for mobile data.** This isn't a knock on any esim provider, it's a structural reality. Rogers, Bell, and Telus have historically had limited competition, and even the esim market reflects this. Budget accordingly, especially if you're coming from home in the UK or Europe where data is significantly cheaper.

**How long is your trip?** A week in Vancouver or Toronto: 3-5GB is comfortable. Two weeks including a Rockies road trip: 10gb of data is safer, especially if you're navigating Google Maps through areas with limited reliable internet. A month of remote work: 20GB. Running out of data mid-road trip in Banff with no Wi-Fi is not a situation you want. There's no get a refund option on most plans once they're active.

**Are you doing city travel or backcountry?** Banff, Jasper, and the Icefields Parkway have high-speed data every day along the main roads and near visitor centers. Deep into backcountry hiking, you'll lose signal from any esim card. Download offline Google Maps before heading out. Your UK sim active alongside a Canadian esim won't help you in the wilderness either.

**Canada plus the United States or other countries?** If your trip crosses the border, Canada-specific prepaid esim plans are the best value for Canada-only time. Multi-country plans from Airalo or Roamless start making sense once you're crossing into the United States for any significant period.

**UK travelers specifically:** If you're coming to Canada from home in the United Kingdom with your UK sim active, your data roaming charges will be eye-watering on most UK contracts. Even if your plan includes roaming, the fair-use caps typically kick in fast and you lose high-speed data every day. Choosing an esim before you fly is the right call. Your uk sim active stays as your backup for calls and texts with family, and you use your esim for internet access. This setup keeps you in touch with family at home without burning through roaming charges.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I get an eSIM for Canada?**

YES. Even if you have international travel coverage on your existing phone service contract, Canadian data roaming is expensive. **How do I get an eSIM for Canada?**

Choose your preferred esim provider from the list above. Buy the esim plan online through their website or mobile app. They'll send a QR code by email or display it in the app. Scan the QR code using your iPhone or Android camera (go to Settings, add eSIM, scan the QR code). Your phone supports esim if it's a modern iPhone or Android (most phones since 2018 support eSIM technology).

**Will my US eSIM work in Canada?**

Your US carrier's esim profile may have data roaming enabled for Canada, but you'll be billed at international roaming rates which can be very expensive. Some US plans include Canada in their coverage at no extra cost (T-Mobile and Verizon both offer this). If your plan does include Canada, use my phone approach with your existing sim card and your esim profile is the easy solution. You can also buy another esim from the list above if your current plan doesn't offer unlimited data on roaming. If not, buying another esim specifically for Canada is the cheapest option. Check your contract before you travel abroad.

**Which is better, Airalo or Yesim?**

For Canada specifically, Airalo is the better choice between these two. Airalo has wider network coverage in Canada, more esim options for Canada, and their mobile app is more polished for managing your esims across international travel. Yesim works but their Canada coverage is less consistent outside major cities. If you need peace of mind on the cellular network across all of Canada including Nova Scotia and western provinces, Airalo is the safer esim card.

**What Canadian networks do eSIMs connect to?**

Most reliable esim brands for Canada connect to Rogers Communications, Bell, and Telus. These three form the entire backbone of Canada's cellular network. Koodo Mobile, Fido, and Public Mobile are sub-brands of Telus and Rogers respectively, so you're effectively on the same network infrastructure either way. No prepaid esim connects to all three simultaneously, but eSIMply's Canadian esim automatically selects the strongest signal available.

**How much data do I need for Canada?**

1-3GB is enough for a long weekend in one city with hotel Wi-Fi and reliable internet in your accommodation. 5GB is right for a week of regular travel. 10gb of data covers two to three weeks including Google Maps navigation, social media, and some remote work. 20GB is what I'd take for a month-long trip with regular video calls or Netflix in the hotel. Canadian data is expensive per gigabyte, so there's less temptation to go bigger than you need to buy. But running out mid-road trip is genuinely inconvenient when the next town with reliable internet is 200km away.

**Can I use a Canada eSIM for calls and texts?**

Most esim cards for Canada are data-only and don't include a local Canadian phone number. For calls and texts, you use WhatsApp, FaceTime, or Signal over your data connection. This covers keeping in touch with family back home in the UK, the United States, or wherever. For calls and texts to local Canadian numbers (taxi companies, restaurants, hotel bookings), you'd need either a local SIM card or a provider like Holafly that includes a phone number. Most travelers manage fine with data-only esim options for Canada.

**Does a Canada eSIM support hotspot tethering?**

Yes. All esim plans from eSIMply, Airalo, Saily, Nomad, and Roamless support hotspot sharing. Useful for road trips when you need laptop internet and there's no café in sight (which in Canada happens a lot). Hotspot use burns through data faster than regular phone use, so account for that when choosing your plan size.
