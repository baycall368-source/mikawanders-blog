## How to Choose the Right eSIM for the United Kingdom

**How long are you going?** A long weekend in London means 1-2GB is enough with hotel wifi at night. Two weeks visiting London, Edinburgh, Oxford, and Bath? Get 5GB minimum. A month-long trip or remote work stint? The 10gb plan covers most situations. The amount of data depends on your usage and whether you relax at your accommodation with wifi calling and hotel internet, or you're out all day streaming maps.

**Do you need unlimited data?** Probably not for a typical UK trip. London has dense wifi at cafés and hotels. Most tourist visitors use 3-5GB over two weeks. If you're doing constant video calls or streaming, unlimited data plans from Nomad are there. For most people, a fixed plan is the smarter and cheaper choice.

**Are you staying in the UK only or going further abroad?** If your destination list takes in Ireland, France, Spain, or Vietnam on a longer round-the-world trip, regional data plans from Airalo or Roamless start making sense. For a UK-only trip, a country-specific eSIM from eSIMply gives better value per GB.

**Do you need a local UK phone number?** All five eSIM providers above are data-only. For WhatsApp, Maps, email, and browsing, you don't need one. If you specifically need to receive SMS to a UK number (bank verification codes, UK service texts), that's when a physical SIM card or a provider with local number options becomes relevant.

**What's public wifi like in the UK?** London has good public Wi-Fi hotspot coverage, particularly in tourist areas and most coffee chains. Outside London it's patchier. In the Scottish countryside, don't count on much of anything outside towns. A beach in Cornwall, a hiking trail in the Lake District, a drive through the Highlands: you NEED your own reliable data connection. Having your own local eSIM for internet access is worth it for navigation if nothing else.

**Can I keep my home number active?** Yes. Modern iPhones and Android devices support dual SIM. Your physical SIM card stays active for calls and sims on your home number, while the eSIM handles mobile data in the UK. No international roaming charges on your home plan, no switching, no drama.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are eSIMs good in the UK?**

YES. The UK has a strong mobile network with great coverage. EE is the largest network with the widest reach, including rural England and most of Scotland. O2 and Vodafone UK are solid in urban areas. 5G speed is excellent in London, Manchester, Birmingham, Edinburgh, and other major cities. Netflix, video calls, maps: all run without issue on the major networks here. For the UK trip most travelers take, connectivity is not a concern. I've used eSIMs across the UK on multiple trips and they work brilliantly.

**What is the best SIM card for visitors to the UK?**

An eSIM is better than a physical SIM card for most visitors. You don't need to find a shop, provide ID, or swap sims at the airport. eSIMply is my pick for the best eSIM for the UK. It connects to the EE network (the best in the UK), costs $7.95 for 3GB/30 days, and you download and install it before you even land. If you specifically want a physical SIM card, Giffgaff is popular but requires postal delivery to a UK address.

**Is Nomad or Holafly better?**

For the UK, Nomad has cheaper unlimited data plans ($11 for 3 days) versus Holafly's higher pricing. Holafly includes a local UK phone number on some plans, which is an advantage if you need telephone calls or SMS on a UK number. For pure data value, Nomad wins. For a local number, Holafly wins. For OVERALL value? eSIMply beats both of them on pricing across all tiers.

**What are the downsides of using an eSIM?**

The main ones: no local phone number (you can't receive UK SMS on a data-only eSIM), you can't delete and reinstall some eSIMs without buying again, and your phone needs to be eSIM compatible and unlocked. Also, if you're in a very rural area with no signal, an eSIM won't help any more than a physical SIM card would. But honestly, for 99% of UK travel, eSIMs are simpler, cheaper, and faster to set up. The activation takes five minutes and you're done.

**How do I install an eSIM for the UK?**

Buy it online before you travel. The eSIM provider sends a QR code by email or in their app. Go to your phone settings, select "add eSIM," scan the QR code, and confirm. About five minutes from checkout to connected. Your phone needs to be eSIM compatible (most iPhones and Android phones made after 2019 are) and network-unlocked. You can delay activation until you land, so validity days don't start counting down at home.

**How much UK data do I need?**

Rough guide: 1-2GB covers a London long weekend with light data usage. 3-5GB handles one to two weeks of standard travel. 10gb covers a month or a remote work trip. Navigation in cities uses data steadily. If you're doing anything rural, download offline maps before you go. I typically use about 4GB on a busy two-week UK trip.

**Can I make calls with a UK data-only eSIM?**

Via apps, yes. WhatsApp or FaceTime and Google Meet all work over data. You can make calls via WhatsApp to anyone, anywhere in the world, for free. Traditional UK telephone calls and SMS to a UK number require either a local SIM card or a provider that includes a local number. For most travelers, app-based calling handles everything. Wifi calling also works at your accommodation.
