## How to Choose the Right eSIM for Saudi Arabia

**How long are you going?** A week in Riyadh or Jeddah with standard tourist use needs 3-5GB data. A longer trip covering multiple cities and AlUla, where you need internet for navigation constantly, means 10GB is the safe package. You don't want to find yourself already traveling with insufficient GB data 24 hours from the nearest top-up option. For remote work or a month-long stay, get the 20GB plan.

**Do you need unlimited data?** Unlimited data plans are available from eSIMply and Saily for Saudi Arabia but at significantly higher costs than fixed GB data packages. Unless you're streaming Netflix daily or on video calls all day, fixed packages are better value for most Saudi Arabia trips.

**Can I use my eSIM as a hotspot in Saudi Arabia?** Yes. All eSIM plans above support hotspot tethering, so you can share your data connection with a laptop. Useful for business travelers or anyone doing remote work from Riyadh or Jeddah. Hotspot use draws down your data allowance faster than regular phone use, so account for it in your plan choice.

**Are you staying in Saudi only or doing the Gulf?** If your trip covers Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Bahrain (a common circuit), regional plans from Airalo or Roamless's multi-country credit balance become more convenient. Country-specific esim plans from eSIMply give better value per GB if you're Saudi-only.

**What about VoIP restrictions?** Saudi Arabia restricts VoIP phone calls on its mobile data network. WhatsApp calling, FaceTime audio, and Skype may be blocked or slower than normal. WhatsApp text messaging works fine. If regular phone calls to your home country are important, plan to use a calling card or a VPN alongside your saudi esim. Saily's built-in NordVPN is convenient for this.

**Do you need a local Saudi number?** None of the saudi arabia esims above include a phone number. For most travel use, you don't need one. Local calling to Saudi businesses, restaurants, or services requires either a physical sim card from an operator at the airport, or Holafly's number-included plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Do eSIMs work in Saudi Arabia?**

Yes. Saudi Arabia has strong 4G and 5G infrastructure on its mobile data network. STC Group and Mobily are the main operators, with STC Group having the wider coverage. The saudi arabia esims on this list connect to these operators and provide a stable internet connection throughout your trip in Riyadh, Jeddah, Medina, and the main tourist areas. Coverage in remote desert is present on main roads but gets slower in very isolated spots.

**Can I use WhatsApp in Saudi Arabia?**

WhatsApp text messaging works fine. Voice and video phone calls via WhatsApp are restricted on the local mobile data network, as are Skype and FaceTime. Standard messaging, Instagram, and most internet browsing works without restriction. For regular phone calls to your home country, use a VPN alongside your esim or use a calling card. Manually enabling a VPN before making calls is the standard workaround.

**Is it cheaper to buy an eSIM than to roam in Saudi Arabia?**

Always. Roaming charges abroad from a United States, European, or Australian carrier can run $15-25 per day in Saudi Arabia. A 10GB saudi esim package for 30 days costs $23.95 on eSIMply. The math is straightforward. You need internet for any meaningful Saudi Arabia trip. Buy it before departure.

**How do I activate an eSIM in Saudi Arabia?**

Prepay online before departure. The esim provider sends a QR code by email. On your smartphone, go to settings, select "add esim" or "add mobile plan," scan the QR code, and confirm. Install your esim and set activation for when you land at the airport. The technical instruction is clear in every provider's mobile app, and the customer support team is available via online chat if you need help. **How much GB data do I need for Saudi Arabia?**

A one-week tourist trip with maps, messaging, and some internet browsing: 3GB is comfortable. Two weeks covering multiple cities: 5-10GB. A longer stay or any remote work: 10GB minimum. Saudi Arabia has good Wi-Fi in hotels and malls. But for navigation in unfamiliar cities, a stable internet connection from your own esim is considerably more reliable than hunting for public Wi-Fi. **Is 5G available in Saudi Arabia?**

Yes. Saudi Arabia has active 5G coverage in Riyadh and Jeddah. STC Group runs one of the more advanced 5G rollouts in the region. Coverage outside the main cities is 4G. For standard traveler use, 4G is fast enough for everything: maps, streaming, video calls (where permitted). The mobile broadband quality is a pleasant surprise for first-time visitors.

**Can I make local calls with a Saudi eSIM?**

Local calling requires either a physical sim card from an operator at the airport, or a service like Holafly that includes a number on their plan. For most tourist trips, you never need local calling capability. WhatsApp messaging and internet access cover virtually everything a traveler needs.
