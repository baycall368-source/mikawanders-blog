## How to Choose the Right eSIM for Romania

**How long are you going?** A week touring Bucharest and Transylvania: 3-5GB is comfortable. Two weeks driving through the country with day hikes: 5-10GB if you're working remotely alongside it. Romania isn't a data-intensive destination for most travelers.

**Do you need unlimited?** For typical Romania tourism, probably not. The country is navigable, the cities are manageable, and accommodation Wi-Fi is generally reliable in the places tourists stay. If you're streaming Netflix from a Bucharest apartment or working on video calls all day, Nomad's unlimited plans are worth looking at (they undercut eSIMply significantly for Romania).

**Can I use my eSIM as a hotspot in Romania?** Yes. All plans above support hotspot tethering. Useful if you need laptop access from a café or your accommodation. Just account for the fact that hotspot use burns through your data allowance faster than regular phone browsing.

**Romania only, or combining with nearby countries?** Romania connects naturally to Bulgaria (Black Sea itinerary), Hungary (Transylvania to Budapest), Moldova, and Serbia. Multi-country Eastern European trips are common. For those, Airalo's regional options or Roamless's credit balance are worth considering.

**Coverage in Transylvania and the Carpathians.** The valley towns and main tourist areas are covered. Bran, Sinaia, Brașov, Sighișoara: all fine. Higher mountain trails and very remote rural areas are spottier, which is normal. Don't rely on constant connectivity for navigation in remote mountain areas.

**Do you need a local number?** Data-only eSIMs cover most travel needs. WhatsApp works for calls and messages. If you need to receive Romanian SMS for specific services, a local physical SIM from Orange RO or Vodafone RO at the airport is an option.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do eSIMs work well in Romania?**

Yes. Romania has surprisingly good mobile infrastructure for the price level of the country. Orange RO, Vodafone RO, and Digi all have strong 4G coverage across the cities and populated areas. 5G is rolling out in Bucharest and Cluj-Napoca. Coverage in Transylvania's main towns is solid. Remote mountain and rural areas are spottier, which is expected.

**Is an eSIM cheaper than roaming in Romania?**

Yes, significantly. International roaming charges frequently exceed $10-15 per day. A 10GB eSIM valid for 30 days costs $16.95 from eSIMply. Romania is also not in the EU, meaning EU free-roaming rules from European carriers don't necessarily apply here. Check your home carrier's terms. An eSIM is almost certainly the better option.

**How much data do I need for Romania?**

A week in Bucharest and a Transylvania road trip: 3-5GB. Two weeks of active travel: 5-10GB. The country has good Wi-Fi in tourist accommodation, so you won't be leaning entirely on your eSIM for heavier data tasks.

**Is there 4G in Transylvania?**

Yes, in the towns and main tourist areas. Brașov, Sibiu, Sighișoara, Sinaia, Bran: all covered adequately. The Transfăgărășan Highway and mountain driving areas can have gaps, but the major stops along the way are covered.

**Can I use an eSIM for remote work in Romania?**

Yes. The 10GB plan from eSIMply at $16.95 handles a month of moderate remote work comfortably. If you're on video calls all day, look at Nomad's unlimited plans, which are cheaper than eSIMply's unlimited tiers for Romania specifically.

**How do I install an eSIM for Romania?**

**What Romanian networks do these eSIMs connect to?**

eSIMply connects to Orange RO, Vodafone RO, and Digi. These are Romania's three main operators, covering cities and most populated areas. The eSIM automatically selects the strongest available signal.

**Do I need to worry about coverage in Bucharest?**

Not at all. Bucharest has excellent 4G and growing 5G coverage. Indoor coverage in hotels, restaurants, and shopping centres is reliable. The city's mobile infrastructure is on par with Western European capitals.
