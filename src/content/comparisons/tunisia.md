## How to Choose the Right eSIM for Tunisia

**How much data do you actually need?** A week of light use in Tunisia (maps, messaging, some browsing, the occasional restaurant lookup) needs about 2-3 GB. Two weeks of more active use including social media and photo uploads, go for 5 GB. If you're a digital nomad working remotely or streaming, the 10 GB plan gives you comfortable headroom. Keep an eye on your data usage through the provider's app so you don't get caught short.

**Fixed plan or pay-as-you-go?** Most travelers are better off with a fixed data plan. You know what you're getting, and eSIMply's pricing is hard to beat. Roamless's balance system makes sense if you're unsure how long you'll stay or if you're crossing into Algeria or Libya (though connectivity options there are a whole separate conversation).

**Do you need a local Tunisian number?** None of the five providers above include a local phone number. You can still use WhatsApp, Signal, and other messaging apps over your internet access for calls and texts. If you need to receive Tunisian SMS for hotel bookings or local services, you'll need a physical SIM card from a Tunisian carrier like Ooredoo or Tunisie Telecom. Getting a prepaid local SIM means visiting a shop and registering with your passport.

**Are you combining Tunisia with other countries?** If your trip includes Morocco, Egypt, or other stops, Airalo's regional plans or Roamless's carry-over balance system offer more flexibility than buying a separate eSIM for each country.

**What about Wi-Fi in Tunisia?** Hotels and cafes in Tunis, Hammamet, and Sousse generally have Wi-Fi, but it ranges from decent to "is this actually connected to anything?" Having your own eSIM data connection means you're not dependent on spotty hotel networks for navigation and transport apps. The connectivity in Tunisia's tourist areas is good enough that you can use your eSIM as your primary internet access and only fall back to Wi-Fi when it's available and actually working.
