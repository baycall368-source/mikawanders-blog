## How to Choose the Right eSIM for Denmark

**How long are you going?** A long weekend in Copenhagen: 1-2GB is comfortable. A week touring Copenhagen and Jutland: 3-5GB. Two weeks with remote work: 10GB handles it. Denmark isn't a particularly data-intensive destination once you're oriented.

**Do you need unlimited?** For most Denmark trips, no. Copenhagen is navigable, accommodation Wi-Fi is generally reliable, and casual travel data use is modest. Unlimited plans carry a significant premium. The exception is if you're doing intensive remote work during a short stay, where Nomad's unlimited plans offer good value.

**Denmark only or Scandinavian circuit?** Sweden and Norway are natural add-ons to a Denmark trip. The Øresund Bridge puts Stockholm within easy reach of Copenhagen. If you're doing the full Scandinavian loop, Airalo's regional plans or Roamless's multi-country credit balance are worth considering over separate country eSIMs.

**Coverage in Jutland and the islands.** The main Jutland cities (Aarhus, Aalborg, Esbjerg) are well covered. Rural Jutland and the smaller Danish islands are generally solid, though very remote areas on Bornholm's west coast or small island communities can be spottier.

**Do you need a local Danish number?** Data-only eSIMs from all five providers don't include a phone number. WhatsApp and internet-based calling handle most travel needs. A local number is only relevant for specific services requiring Danish SMS verification.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do eSIMs work well in Denmark?**

Yes. Denmark has excellent mobile infrastructure. TDC, Telenor DK, and 3 DK all have strong 4G and 5G coverage across Copenhagen and the country. Coverage extends well across Jutland and the main Danish islands. eSIMs from the providers above connect reliably across Denmark. Data speeds are consistently good, even outside the major cities.

**Is an eSIM cheaper than roaming in Denmark?**

Yes. International roaming charges can run $10-20 per day on many plans. A 10GB eSIM valid for 30 days costs $16.95 from eSIMply. Even EU free roaming has fair-use limits for extended stays. An eSIM is consistently better value for anything beyond a day or two.

**How much data do I need for Denmark?**

A long weekend in Copenhagen: 1-2GB. A week touring Copenhagen and Aarhus: 3GB. Two weeks including Jutland and island travel: 5GB. Remote work for a month: 10GB.

**Is coverage good in Aarhus and Jutland?**

Yes. Aarhus has good 4G coverage on all major networks. Jutland is well covered in the urban areas and most populated parts. Remote countryside areas and small islands may be spottier, but for standard tourist itineraries, coverage is solid.

**Can I use my eSIM as a hotspot in Denmark?**

Yes. All plans above support hotspot use. Useful for working on a laptop in Copenhagen. Hotspot drains data faster than phone-only use, so factor that into your eSIM plan choice if you're planning to tether regularly. Netflix and other streaming at night over the hotel Wi-Fi is always an option too, which saves your mobile data for daytime use.

**How do I install an eSIM for Denmark?**

Buy online, get QR code, go to phone settings, add new eSIM, scan code. Under five minutes. Set activation for your arrival date. Phone must be eSIM-compatible and carrier-unlocked. eSIM technology is built into most smartphones made after 2018, so the process is straightforward.

**What Danish networks do these eSIMs use?**

eSIMply connects to TDC, Telenor DK, and 3 DK. These are Denmark's major operators with strong nationwide coverage. Your eSIM automatically selects the strongest available signal.

**Does eSIM work on the bridge to Sweden?**

Yes. The Øresund Bridge and connecting tunnel between Copenhagen and Malmö are well covered by Danish networks on the Danish side and Swedish networks on the Swedish side. Your eSIM will maintain connectivity throughout the crossing.
