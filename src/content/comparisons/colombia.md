Two weeks across Bogotá, the coffee region, and Cartagena means 3-5GB depending on how much you navigate and use data on the go. A month of remote work means 5GB minimum, probably more.

**Note eSIMply's limited plan range.** eSIMply only goes up to 5GB for Colombia. If you need 10GB or 20GB, you'll need Airalo, Nomad, or Roamless. For most week-to-two-week trips, 5GB is enough. For longer stays or heavy remote work, check the alternatives.

**Medellín as a base.** Medellín is one of the most popular digital nomad destinations in Latin America. If you're spending a month there working remotely, a 5GB plan probably doesn't cover you. Factor in video calls, streaming, and the temptation to work from cafés with unreliable Wi-Fi. Plan accordingly.

**Colombia plus Peru or Ecuador?** Multi-country South America trips benefit from Airalo's regional approach or Roamless's balance system. Country-specific plans from eSIMply give the best per-GB value for Colombia alone.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do eSIMs work well in Colombia?**

Yes, in cities and main tourist areas. Claro, Movistar, and Tigo all have solid 4G coverage across Bogotá, Medellín, Cali, Cartagena, and the coffee region. 5G is rolling out in major cities. Coverage drops in remote rural areas, jungle, and the more isolated parts of the Amazon basin. For the vast majority of tourist itineraries, eSIMs work reliably.

**Is an eSIM cheaper than roaming in Colombia?**

Yes. International roaming from a US, European, or Australian plan in Colombia typically costs $5-15 per day. A 5GB eSIM from eSIMply for 30 days costs $14.95 total. That's less than one day of roaming on most plans. Buy an eSIM.

**Which Colombian network is best for travelers?**

Claro has the widest 4G coverage across Colombia and is the strongest network for most tourist destinations. Movistar is solid in cities. Tigo fills in specific regional gaps. Most eSIM providers connect to Claro as the primary network. eSIMply connects to the strongest available network in your location.

**Is Colombia safe to use my phone?**

Phone theft is a real concern in parts of Bogotá and Cartagena, as it is in many Latin American cities. Use your phone with awareness in crowded areas and avoid having it out unnecessarily in tourist-heavy spots. This is a physical security note, not an eSIM note, but worth knowing. Your eSIM data isn't at risk from this, just the physical device.

**How do I buy an eSIM for Colombia?**

Buy online before you travel. The provider sends a QR code. Go to your phone settings, add eSIM, scan the code, set activation for when you land. Confirm your phone is eSIM-compatible and unlocked. Most phones made after 2018 are compatible.

**How much data do I need for Colombia?**

1-2GB for a long weekend in one city with Wi-Fi at accommodation. 3-5GB for two weeks of regular travel. 5GB+ for a month including remote work. For heavy video call days, you'll burn 1-2GB per day easily. Track your usage in the eSIM app and top up if needed.

**What about connectivity in Tayrona National Park?**

Variable. Near the park entrance and some campsites, you'll have signal. On the hiking trails and at more remote beaches, expect gaps. Download offline maps for the park before you arrive. Signal returns in the nearby towns of Santa Marta and Taganga.

**Does Colombia have good coffee shop Wi-Fi?**

Yes, often surprisingly good. Medellín in particular has a thriving café culture with reliable Wi-Fi. But "reliable" is relative: you still want your own eSIM data connection as a backup for important calls or navigation. Don't bet your working day on a café router. And for evenings back at the hostel, the 4G data speeds are sufficient for Netflix and video calls if the accommodation Wi-Fi is patchy.
